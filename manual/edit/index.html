Draw, sketch and edit your image

After opening an existing image or creating a new empty image, a window will show up automatically. You can draw, sketch, or edit your image at here. The window is divided into several parts as shown in the screenshot below:

  • The top bar on the top.
  • The attributes panel below the toolbar.
  • The layers/objects panel on the left.
  • The canvas in the center includiging the rulers.
  • The bottom bar on the bottom.

Top bar

The top bar contains the following parts as shown in the screenshot above:

  • The tools bar.
    The tools bar is used to select the tool you want to use. You can choose one of them as you want. You can also choose a tool by choosing the corresponding menu commands from the Tool menu in the top menu bar. Besides, the menu commands support keyboard shortcuts.
    To know more about all tools, please refer to Use tools.

  • The edit button.
    When you click this button, it will pop up a menu which has many regular edit commands which are available when you select some objects or some part on your imgage. The available commands automatically change based on the object you are currently selecting. These commands are also available in the top menu bar's Object menu and most of them support keyboard shortcuts.

  • The rotate & resize button.
    This button is available when you select some objects or some part on your imgage. It allows you to rotate or resize your selected objects or image part. The command is also available in the top menu bar's Object menu and it supports keyboard shortcuts.

  • The undo/redo control.
    You can undo or redo your actions by clicking this control. It is also available by choosing the Edit > Undo or Redo menu in the top menu bar. Besides, the menu commands support keyboard shortcuts.

  • The zoom control. You can zoom your image in/out/to its actural size by clicking this control. They are also available by choosing the View > Zoom In, Zoom Out, Actual Size and Zoom ot Fit menu in the top menu bar. Besides, the menu commands support keyboard shortcuts.

  • The share button.
    You can share or open your image with other apps and services by clicking this button.

Attributes panel

The attributes panel shows the attributes of your current selected tool or objects. The available attributes are different for different tools and objects. You can modify the attributes of your objects by modifying the attributes in this panel. To get more information about the attributes, please refer to Modify attributes.

Layers/Objects panel

The layers/objects panel is on the left side of the window. It shows the layers/objects in your image. You can select a layer or an object by clicking on it. The selected layers/objects will be highlighted. You can also manage your layers/objects at here, includiging delete, merge, unmerge, arrange, rename, lock, toggle visible etc. To know more about how to manage your layers/objects, please refer to Manage and edit layers/objects.

Canvas

The canvas is in the center of the window. It includes the rulers and the image canvas where you can draw and sketch.
You can change the ruler unit by choosing the View > Ruler Units menu.
You can change the origin of the rulers by choosing the View > Ruler Start Point menu.
You can also show or hide the canvas grid by choosing the View > Show Canvas Grid menu. To configure the canvas grid, please choose the View > Configure Canvas Grid menu.

Bottom bar

The bottom bar contains the following parts:

  • Image size.
    It show the current size of your canvas/image. The unit is the same as the ruler unit.

  • Zoom.
    It shows the current zoom level of your image. You can zoom in/out by choosing an item from its pop-up menu.

  • Cursor position in your image.
    It shows the current cursor position in your image. The unit is the same as the ruler unit.

  • Canvas background.
    It allows you to change the background of your canvas. There are two types of canvas background: checkerboard and solid color. You can choose one of them by choosing an item from its pop-up menu. The default is checkerboard.

    Tips

    You should note that the canvas background just represents the background of the canvas. It does not affect the background of your image. In other works, it won't appear in your image. It is only used to help you to see the transparent part of your image. If your image is not transparent, you won't see the canvas background. If there are transparent parts in your image, you will see the canvas background in those parts.

  • Image background color.
    It allows you to change the background of your image. Different with the canvas background, the image background color will affect your image. It will appear in your image.

    Tips

    If you don't want to use a image background color, you can choose the clear color from the poped-up color panel.

  • Resize image/canvas.
    It allows you to resize your image/canvas. You can also do this by choosing the Image > Adjust Size... menu. To get more information about resizing image/canvas, please refer to Resize image.
